5.  You are considering the purchase of several bonds and are concerned about the sensitivity of the bonds in what you believe will be a volatile marketplace for several years.

a.     If two bonds being considered each have a coupon of 8% but one matures in 10 years and one in 5 years, which will be more sensitive to changes in interest rates?

Two bonds with same coupon (8%)

  • Bond 1: 10-year maturity, 8% coupon
  • Bond 2: 5-year maturity, 8% coupon

Since both have the same coupon rate, the deciding factor is maturity.

  • The 10-year bond has a longer maturity → higher duration → more sensitive to interest rate changes.

The 10-year bond will be more sensitive.

 

b.     If two bonds being considered each have a maturity of 18 years but one has a coupon of 9% while the other has a coupon of 6%, which will be more sensitive to changes in interest rates?

Two bonds with same maturity (18 years)

·       Bond 1: 18-year maturity, 9% coupon

·       Bond 2: 18-year maturity, 6% coupon

Since both have the same maturity, the deciding factor is the coupon size.

·       The lower coupon bond pays less cash earlier and more weight is on the final lump-sum payment → higher duration → more sensitive to interest rate changes.

The 18-year, 6% coupon bond will be more sensitive.
