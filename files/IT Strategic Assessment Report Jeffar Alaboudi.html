1.             Executive Summary

For my Strategic Assessment project, I have chosen a not-for-profit organization called Shia of Louisville (Shia Youth Group). This organization is concerned with engaging and supporting Shia Muslim youth by helping them grow closer to Islam while also building their sense of community. This group provides a platform for the youth to learn, socialize, and engage in religious, educational, and service-oriented activities.

  The purpose of Shia of Louisville is to empower youth through Islamic education and involvement. The organization is geared towards instilling good moral character, increasing religious knowledge, and fostering unity among youth and their families. This is achieved through religious events, discussion nights, social gatherings, volunteer work, and partnerships with the local mosque. The Shia of Louisville Instagram page also tracks the development of the organization over the years, showcasing the changes in their programs and outreach efforts.

  To obtain the information required for this report, I would closely work with the youth leaders, organizers, and volunteers who help in planning and organizing activities for the group. This would include the youth coordinator and senior volunteers who are responsible for events and communication. In addition, the Instagram account of the organization will be an important source of information, both historical and operational, that will provide examples of previous events, attendance, and community engagement. These sources will provide enough information about the structure and operations of the organization and areas were improvements, especially in information technology, can be suggested.

 

2.             History and Purpose

2.1           History of the <Organization>

Shia of Louisville, also known as the Shia Youth Group, is a not-for-profit organization whose objective is to assist and involve Shia Muslim youth in the Louisville area. The organization was formed to enable young people to enhance their relationship with Islam while also developing good relationships with other people in society. However, the organization has since grown to become an active youth organization focused on education and faith.

  Since the formation of Shia of Louisville, the organization has been involved in different religious, social, and volunteer events that assist youth in developing both spiritually and socially. These events include discussion nights, educational events, volunteer work, and social events that allow the youth to form friendships as well as strengthen their faith by attending events that the organization has collaborated with the mosque to hold.

 The organization over the years been able to grow by adding new programs and increasing participation among young people within the community. This can be seen by looking at the organization’s Instagram page, which shows different activities that have taken place over the years. This social media page offers a glimpse into how the organization has been able to grow and change its activities to meet the interests and needs of young people. Shia of Louisville has over the years been able to function as a hub for young people’s leadership and education.

 

2.2           Purpose of the <Organization>

   The purpose of Shia of Louisville (Shia Youth Group) is to support, guide, and empower Shia Muslim youth by offering opportunities for their spiritual education, community engagement, and personal development. Shia of Louisville aims at creating an environment where young people can enhance their relationship with Islam while at the same time developing significant associations with other people within the same community. By focusing on the spiritual and personal development of the youth, the organization enables them to develop a strong sense of identity, responsibility, and belonging.

  One of the main objectives of the organization is to instill Islamic values and moral ethics in the youth of the country. The organization, Shia of Louisville, provides the youth members of the organization the opportunity to learn more about their faith through educational discussions, religious programs, and mentorship opportunities. These programs not only allow the youth to ask questions and gain a deeper understanding of the Islamic faith but also enhance their confidence levels. The organization also seeks to unite youth through various social activities. Such activities include discussion nights, social events, sports events, and volunteer activities. These events help build good friendships and teamwork. They provide a balanced environment where youth can develop both spiritually and socially, while remaining grounded in their local communities. Another key purpose of Shia of Louisville is to enhance the relationship between youths, families, and the local Muslim community. The organization works with the local mosque to help youths participate in activities that are important to the community in a positive manner. The organization has provided an important space for youths in the Shia Muslim community in Louisville. It has enabled youths to take leadership roles in activities that are important to the community.

3.             Management and Business Processes.

Shia of Louisville (Shia Youth Group) is a community-based organization that works primarily through volunteers and youth leaders. The main purpose of Shia of Louisville is to engage youth in the Shia Muslim community through providing opportunities to be involved in religious activities, community activities, and social growth. The organization is at a smaller scale due to its community-based operations; therefore, it has informal management and operational processes through communication with volunteers and youth leaders. The organization does not have any information management system or technology to facilitate its operations; rather, it works through manual processes through communication with each other through conversations, group communication, and social media communication. While this type of structure has been successful in helping the organization function on a small level, there are potential problems that can arise in organization, communication, and planning. As the organization continues to expand and more youth are involved in participating in the events and activities, the need for better management tools is becoming increasingly important. The following sections will discuss the major operational practices that help the organization carry out its mission. The operational practices include planning, communicating, organizing, and community outreach.

3.1           Practice 1 Event Planning and Coordination

   One of the most important operational activities in Shia of Louisville is event planning. The organization is known to regularly organize different activities that are geared towards engaging the youth and helping them feel a sense of belonging to Islam and the community in general. The activities that are organized may include educational activities, religious activities, lectures, community service activities, and social activities such as sports or discussions. Currently, the process of organizing events is informal. For instance, youth leaders and volunteers usually communicate their ideas for events in the future through a meeting or through a messaging app. After an event idea is generated, various responsibilities are assigned to various volunteers who assist in preparing event materials, creating awareness about the event, preparing the event location, and so on. Since the organization is run on a volunteer basis, communication of responsibilities is usually done informally. The challenge that may come because of this process is that some of the vital information may not be well documented. As a result, the leaders may be forced to rely on memory to ensure that all the responsibilities are well taken care of. Although the system is working as it is because of the volunteers’ commitment, the introduction of a new system of planning events may greatly enhance the planning of events because the leaders may be able to create the events’ schedules as well as assign the tasks.

3.2           Practice 2 Communication with Members

 Communication is another important process that enables an organization to achieve its mission. The youth group needs to communicate regularly with its members regarding various events and educational opportunities within the community. Currently, communication is done through various social media platforms, especially Instagram. In addition, personal communication is done through personal messages within the community. Instagram is an important tool for the organization’s outreach and communication strategy. Flyers and announcements for events are posted on the organization’s Instagram account. This keeps people updated about events that are coming up. Information is also included in the posts about events, for instance, when an event is scheduled to take place, where it is taking place, and a brief description of the program. Social media is also a tool for reaching a wider audience and encouraging new youth members to attend events. However, there are certain limitations to relying on these social media tools for communication. One such limitation is that there is no membership list to determine the people receiving announcements or attending meetings. There is no easy way for leaders to send messages to certain groups or maintain a list of members they should contact. There is a high probability that communication depends on certain informal networks within the community. This process could be made better by having a centralized form of communication. This can be achieved by having a system that has a database of members, sends announcements, and allows members to register for different events. This would ensure that every member gets information they need while at the same time making the process of communication more efficient.

3.3           Practice 3 Volunteer Coordination

  The role played by volunteers in the success of Shia of Louisville cannot be overstated. This is because the organization is community-based and nonprofit in nature. Therefore, it relies on volunteers who are willing to donate their time and effort to the success of youth programs and other activities within the community. Currently, volunteer management is done in an informal manner through youth leaders and coordinators. Volunteers can be reached through their social connections or announcements during events in various groups. Once a volunteer agrees to assist in a specific activity, their roles are communicated through face-to-face conversations or messaging applications. Tasks can include preparing spaces for events, preparing presentations, and distributing materials, among others. One of the biggest challenges of this approach is the lack of a structured system for volunteers. There is no central database to track volunteers' participation, skills, and availability. This means that the organizers will not know who to turn to for the next event and what skills they can bring to the organization. A system for managing volunteers can be developed, which can aid in improving this process, as it can help leaders keep records of their volunteers, their assigned duties, and their contributions to the organization, which can be helpful in recognizing their contributions appropriately.

3.4           Practice 4 Community Engagement and Outreach

 Community engagement seems to be one of the key aspects in the organization’s mission statement. The mission of Shia of Louisville is to serve the youth but also to improve relations within the larger Muslim community. The organization works with the mosque and other community members to arrange events and encourage community participation from other families and community leaders. Outreach activities usually involve promoting programs that encourage youth involvement, as well as supporting activities within the community. For example, the organization may plan activities such as volunteering, lectures, or social events that bring together members of different age groups within the community. This will create a sense of oneness among the members, while at the same time encouraging the youths to become actively involved within the community. Most of these outreaches are currently done via social media, announcements, and personal networks. While these methods are highly effective for dissemination of information, they don’t offer a framework for measuring engagement and participation. The leaders may not have accurate data on attendance rates, participation rates, and the effectiveness of certain programs. Having such a system for monitoring participation and engagement within events can assist the organization in effectively assessing their outreach activities. This system can help leaders obtain feedback, monitor participation trends, and identify ways of improving their activities.

3.5           Practice 5 Information and Record Management

Information management is another key process in managing an organization. Just like other small nonprofit groups in society, Shia of Louisville lacks a formal system for managing organizational data. Information is usually stored in personal messages or documents and social media posts. This absence of formal management of records may also make it hard to keep records regarding the activities that have been carried out in the organization over time. For instance, keeping records regarding attendance, volunteer activities, or keeping records regarding past activities may involve searching through messages or social media updates. However, this may become inefficient if the organization grows and more activities are carried out. As part of this strategic assessment, there is an opportunity to enhance this process by developing a centralized digital system. This digital system could potentially hold information such as information on an event, information on volunteers, and information on communication in one place. Additionally, it could potentially hold tools for scheduling an event and tools for recording organizational activities. The organization could also become more efficient and better support its long-term growth by implementing a centralized information system. Such an improvement could also help ensure that valuable organizational knowledge is preserved and is easily accessible to future organizational leaders and volunteers.
