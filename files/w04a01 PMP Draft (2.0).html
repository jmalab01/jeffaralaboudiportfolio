1.   EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Hope Buss Revamped Website Project is undertaken to modernize and enhance the existing Hope Buss nonprofit website to better support community outreach, donations, volunteer engagement, and public awareness. The project is managed by the Derby City Developers project group and focuses on delivering a secure, accessible, and user-friendly web platform aligned with stakeholder requirements. This Project Management Plan defines how the project will be executed, monitored, controlled, and closed.

 

 

2.   PROJECT MANAGEMENT APPROACH AND GOVERNANCE

The project will follow a structured project management approach. Governance will be provided by the Project Sponsor, with oversight from the Project Manager. The Derby City Developers team is responsible for planning, execution, monitoring, and delivery of project deliverables. Project decisions will be documented and communicated to stakeholders.

 

Assumptions include availability of stakeholder feedback and use of standard web technologies. Constraints include academic timelines, limited budget, and defined scope boundaries.

 

 

 

2.1          PROJECT SCOPE

The project scope includes the design, development, testing, and deployment of a revamped public-facing website for The Hope Buss. The scope is defined by the approved Statement of Work and Requirements Traceability Matrix.

 

 

2.2          PROJECT CHARTER

Document (As found in Appendix):

Paragraph: This project charter outlines the plan to revamp The Hope Buss’s website in order to address key shortcomings in usability, accessibility, and audience engagement that limit the organization’s ability to communicate its mission and attract donors and volunteers PC Final The improved website introduces a modern, user-friendly, and accessible design, along with integrated donation, volunteering, and form plug-ins, which will directly enhance how the users interact with the organization online. These improvements matter because, as a nonprofit, The Hope Buss relies heavily on public trust, visibility, and ease of engagement. A clear, accessible website increases credibility and removes barriers for users who want to donate or get involved. Additionally, the structured scope, timeline, and low-cost technology choices (such as WordPress and select plug-ins) ensure the project is realistic and sustainable within a nonprofit budget, while still delivering meaningful gains in outreach, engagement, and long-term organizational effectiveness.

 

Source: 6 Free Project Charter Templates with Expert Tips | Smartsheet

 

2.3          STATEMENT OF WORK

Document (As found in Appendix):

Paragraph: The SOW builds on the project charter by clearly defining the responsibilities, scope, and expectations for the Hope Buss website revamp, helping ensure alignment between Derby City Developers and the project sponsor. The SOW improves the overall project structure by formally outlining what work is included such as planning, design, development, testing, and deployment while also identifying what is outside the scope, which reduces confusion and prevents scope creep. These improvements are important because they set clear boundaries, timelines, and deliverables, allowing both the development team and The Hope Buss to understand their roles and responsibilities from the start. By documenting the expectations upfront, the SOW increases accountability, minimizes project risk, and helps keep the project on schedule and within budget, which is especially critical for a nonprofit organization with limited resources. All in all, the SOW strengthens project execution by turning high level goals into clearly defined, actionable commitments that support a successful and efficient website redesign.

 

Source: SOW Guide 02

2.9          CHANGE MANAGEMENT PLAN

All change requests must be submitted to the Project Manager. Changes will be analyzed for impact to scope, schedule, and cost. Approval authority resides with the Project Sponsor. Approved changes will be documented and communicated to all group members.

 

 

2.10      PROJECT SCOPE MANAGEMENT PLAN 

The Project Manager is responsible for managing scope in alignment with the approved requirements and SOW. Scope verification will occur at major milestones, and final approval rests with the Project Sponsor.

7.   RISK MANAGEMENT PLAN

Risks will be identified, analyzed, and prioritized throughout the project lifecycle. Mitigation strategies and contingency plans will be documented and reviewed regularly.

 

Document (As found in Appendix):

Paragraph: This Requirements Management Plan explains how requirements for the Hope Buss Revamped Website Project will be identified, organized, tracked, and controlled throughout the project lifecycle, ensuring the final system meets stakeholder needs and project goals. Compared to a less structured approach, this plan improves the project by introducing a clear, step-by-step requirements management process that includes identification, analysis, documentation, prioritization, and ongoing change control. These improvements are important because they reduce misunderstandings, prevent uncontrolled changes, and ensure that critical features such as donations, volunteer management, and information sharing are delivered first. The RMP strengthens accountability and traceability, making it easier to verify that all requirements are implemented and tested. Overall, this plan enhances project quality and efficiency by keeping development aligned with stakeholder expectations, budget constraints, and the nonprofit mission of effective community outreach.

7.1          RISK TRACEABILITY MATRIX

Document (As found in Appendix):

Paragraph: This Requirements Traceability Matrix (RTM) documents the direct linkage between project requirements and their corresponding design elements, development tasks, testing activities, and final deliverables for the Hope Buss Revamped Website Project. It improves the project by ensuring that every requirement identified by stakeholders is tracked throughout the entire project lifecycle from initial approval to implementation and validation, so nothing critical is missed or overlooked. This is important because it increases accountability and transparency, making it easy to verify that all required features, such as donation tools, volunteer management, and accessibility requirements, are properly built and tested. The RTM also helps manage change more effectively by showing the impact of any requirement updates on scope, schedule, and testing. All in all, this matrix strengthens quality control and alignment with project goals, ensuring the final website fully supports The Hope Buss’s mission while staying within scope and expectations.
